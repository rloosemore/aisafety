> Framework: what are people "hiring" AISafety.org to do for them?

## Job 1: Orient Me in the Field
**Who:** Newcomers (students, career-changers, curious professionals)
**Situation:** "I keep hearing about AI safety but the field seems huge and fragmented. I don't know where to start."
**Desired outcome:** Understand the landscape, find a starting point relevant to my background
**Current alternatives:** Reddit threads, Twitter, scattered blog posts, asking friends

## Job 2: Keep Me Current
**Who:** Active practitioners, researchers, policy professionals
**Situation:** "I work in AI safety but I can't track everything. I miss important developments."
**Desired outcome:** Know what matters this week without reading everything
**Current alternatives:** Twitter, mailing lists, conference talks, word of mouth

## Job 3: Help Me Decide
**Who:** Engineers, product managers, policy advisors
**Situation:** "I need to make a decision about AI safety for my work and I need frameworks, not just theory."
**Desired outcome:** Actionable checklists, evaluation templates, decision frameworks
**Current alternatives:** Internal documents, consulting, improvising

## Job 4: Help Me Teach
**Who:** Educators, workshop leaders, team leads
**Situation:** "I need to explain AI safety to others but creating materials from scratch is too much work."
**Desired outcome:** Ready-to-use teaching materials, slide decks, reading lists
**Current alternatives:** DIY from papers and blog posts

## Job 5: Help Me Speak Credibly
**Who:** Journalists, policymakers, executives
**Situation:** "I need to discuss AI safety publicly without looking ignorant or alarmist."
**Desired outcome:** Accurate, nuanced understanding I can communicate confidently
**Current alternatives:** Wikipedia, quick Google searches, asking contacts
